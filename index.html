What is the Feldenkrais Method®?
The Feldenkrais Method® of somatic education, i.e., relating to the body, uses gentle movement and directed attention to help people become more aware of how they are moving and learn new and more effective ways to function. You will be guided with gentle touch and/or verbal direction to become more aware of habits and tensions, develop a clearer sense of skeletal support, and explore new possibilities.

The Feldenkrais Method® was developed by Dr. Moshe Feldenkrais, physicist, mechanical engineer, and martial artist, based on principles of physics, biomechanics, learning, and human development.

“Make the impossible possible, the possible easy, and the easy elegant.”
— Moshe Feldenkrais, PhD

About Elizabeth

About Elizabeth

I had been in a motor vehicle accident and had pain in my left hip and right shoulder. Treatments focused on one area, and when the pain in the other area got worse, the treatment focused on that area. The first time I had a Feldenkrais® session, I was told that it was not a problem with my shoulder OR my hip, but the relationship between the two and the organization of my WHOLE body -- not the isolated parts. The pain disappeared and I was able to sleep on my stomach again and turn my head with ease. Although I no longer have ongoing pain, I now realize that if I ever do experience any discomfort, it’s a reminder that I have choices about how to move more comfortably.

An avid swimmer, I also noticed that after experiencing the Feldenkrais Method®, I was swimming as fast as I swam 30 years previously and felt like I could swim effortlessly and with greater endurance.

In addition to being a Guild Certified Feldenkrais PractitionerCM and IOPS (Ideal Organization and Profound Strength) Practitioner, I am a Licensed Massage Practitioner and Jin Shin Do® Acupressurist.

I also have a M.S. degree in Industrial Engineering from the University of Wisconsin and have worked as a Process Improvement Coach, specializing in health care.
