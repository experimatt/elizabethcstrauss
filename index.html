What is Feldenkrais?

The Feldenkrais Method® of somatic education, i.e., relating to the body, uses gentle movement and directed attention to help people become more aware of how they are moving and learn new and more effective ways to move. You will be guided with gentle touch and/or verbal direction to become more aware of habits and tensions and explore new possibilities.

Benefits include increased ease and range of motion, improved strength, flexibility, and coordination, as well as a holistic integration of body and mind. People often sense a reduction in pain, an overall feeling of well-being, including ease in breathing and a clearer sense of skeletal support, resulting in increased vitality and energy, as well as calm composure and presence.

The lessons enhance human functioning in daily life, including walking efficiently, improving one’s posture (while sitting, using a computer, or standing), safely reaching for or lifting objects, and performing other physical activities with greater ease. As you attend to improving the quality of movement, unnecessary muscular tensions throughout the body can reorganize and de-contract. People are often amazed at the quick and clear changes that occur through the neuromuscular re-patterning. These improvements will often enhance your thinking, emotional regulation, problem solving capabilities, and the quality of how you move through life.

The Feldenkrais Method®, was developed by Dr. Moshe Feldenkrais, physicist, mechanical engineer, and martial artist, based on principles of physics, biomechanics, learning, and human development.

“The aim [of the Feldenkrais Method] is a person that is organized to move with minimum effort and maximum efficiency, not through muscular strength, but through increased consciousness of how movement works.”

“Make the impossible possible, the possible easy, and the easy elegant.”

— Moshe Feldenkrais, PhD
